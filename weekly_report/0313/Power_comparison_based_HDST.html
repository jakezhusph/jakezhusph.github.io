Three Patterns Examined for Power Comparison

Figure 0: Three Patterns (A): Layer E (B): Layer ONL (C): Layer GL

Pattern Plots

Power Figures (Fig.1-4): numZero = 1000, sigZero = 200.
In this setting, KDC does not fare well in the Layer E (A) across all sample size. For the second pattern (B), the power trend is not clear: high in 20X resolution, but decrease at 15X resolution, and then bounce back at 10X resolution. For the third pattern (C), KDC reaches highest power at 15X resolution, and spatialDE maximum power is at 20X resolution. SPARK result is very stable across all setting.

Power Figures II (Fig.5-8), numZero = 500, sigZero = 100.
In this setting, KDC gains power in the Layer E (A) as the sample size increases. For the other two patterns (B) and (C), KDC attains high power.
Both SPARK and SpatialDE attains full power in almost all setting. Though the ratio of numZero/sigZero are the same between these two settings, the 500/100 version probably has less noise.

Power Figures III (Fig.9-Fig.13), numZero = 2000, sigZero = 200.
In this setting, KDC gains more power in the Layer E (A) as the sample size increases at the beginning, and reaches the maximum power at 10X resolution and then decreases at afterwards. The KDC power trend in the Layer ONL (B) is clear if ignore 15X resolution, maximum power at 5X. In the Layer GL (C), KDC reaches maximum power at 15X resolution and slightly decrease afterwards.
For the SpatialDE, the maximum power reaches at 20X resolution for all three patterns, and vanishes quickly and loses all power at 5X resolution. There is no results available for SPARK at resolution 10X and 5X (but presumably would be stable based on the previous two setting).
